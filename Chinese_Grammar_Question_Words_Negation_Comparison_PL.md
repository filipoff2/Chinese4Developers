# Chinese Grammar: Question Words & Negation
## Comparison with Polish (English Explanations)

---

## 1. General Structural Difference

### Chinese
- **Analytic language** (no cases, no conjugation)
- Grammar relies heavily on:
  - Word order
  - Particles
  - Context

### Polish
- **Fusional language**
- Meaning is encoded through:
  - Case endings
  - Verb conjugation
  - Question words moved to sentence start

**Key difference:**  
Chinese usually **does not change word order** for questions or negation,  
while Polish often does.

---

## 2. Question Words (Wh-Questions)

### 2.1 Core Rule in Chinese

> **Chinese question words stay in the same place as the answer.**

Polish and English move question words to the beginning.

---

### 2.2 Examples of Question Words

| Meaning | Chinese | Pinyin | Polish |
|------|--------|--------|--------|
| who | 谁 | shéi | kto |
| what | 什么 | shénme | co |
| where | 哪里 / 在哪儿 | nǎlǐ / zài nǎr | gdzie |
| when | 什么时候 | shénme shíhou | kiedy |
| why | 为什么 | wèishénme | dlaczego |
| how | 怎么 | zěnme | jak |

---

### 2.3 Sentence Examples

#### ✅ Statement
- **Chinese:**  
  我**在北京**工作。  
  *(I work in Beijing.)*

- **Polish:**  
  Pracuję **w Pekinie**.

---

#### ✅ Question (Where?)

- **Chinese:**  
  你**在哪里**工作？  
  *(You work where?)*

- **Polish:**  
  **Gdzie** pracujesz?

✅ Same word order in Chinese  
❌ Question word fronted in Polish

---

#### ✅ Question (What?)

- **Chinese:**  
  你喜欢**什么**？  
  *(You like what?)*

- **Polish:**  
  **Co** lubisz?

---

### 2.4 Yes/No Questions in Chinese

Chinese often adds **吗 (ma)** at the end:

- 你喜欢中国吗？  
  *(Do you like China?)*

Polish usually:
- Changes **intonation**
- OR adds **czy**

- Czy lubisz Chiny?

## 3. Negation

### 3.1 Main Negation Words in Chinese

| Negation | Use | Polish Equivalent |
|-------|----|------------------|
| 不 (bù) | habitual, future, general | nie |
| 没 (méi) | past actions, have not | nie / nie ma |

---

### 3.2 不 (bù) — General / Present / Future

#### Chinese
- 我**不**喜欢咖啡。  
  *(I do not like coffee.)*

#### Polish
- **Nie** lubię kawy.

✅ Similar logic, but:
- Chinese does not change the verb
- Polish verb is conjugated

---

### 3.3 没 (méi) — Past or Non-existence

#### Chinese
- 我**没**去中国。  
  *(I did not go to China.)*

- 我**没有**钱。  
  *(I do not have money.)*

#### Polish
- **Nie** pojechałem do Chin.  
- **Nie mam** pieniędzy.

⚠️ Important:
- Chinese negates **the action**, not verb tense
- Polish marks past through verb conjugation

---

### 3.4 Negation + 有 (to have)

Chinese:
- ✅ 没有  
- ❌ 不有 (incorrect)

| Chinese | Polish | English |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 我没有车 | Nie mam samochodu | I don't have a car |

---

## 4. Double Negation (Important Difference)

### Chinese
- Double negation is **rare**
- Means **emphasis**, not rules

### Polish
- Double negation is **mandatory**

#### Polish
- **Nikt nie** przyszedł.  
  *(Literally: Nobody not came)*

#### Chinese
- **没有人**来。  
  *(Not-have person come)*

✅ Polish requires two negatives  
✅ Chinese allows **only one**

---

## 5. Summary Table

| Feature | Chinese | Polish |
|------|--------|--------|
| Verb conjugation | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| Cases | ❌ No | ✅ Yes |
| Question word position | In-place | Sentence start |
| Yes/No questions | 吗 / intonation | Czy / intonation |
| Past tense | Context + 没 | Verb form |
| Double negation | ❌ Rare | ✅ Required |

---

## 6. Learning Tip for Polish Speakers

> When learning Chinese, **stop thinking in cases and conjugation**.  
> Think instead:
- WHO does WHAT
- WHERE it happens
- WHEN it happens

And keep the **same sentence order**, adding particles where needed.
