# Common Chinese Characters (Radicals & Components) Used to Form Other Characters

In Chinese, many characters are built from **radicals (部首)** or **common components**, which combine semantic meaning and/or pronunciation. Below are some of the **most common characters/components** that appear repeatedly in other characters.

---

## 1. 人 / 亻 (Person)
**Meaning:** person, human  
**Role:** Semantic (related to people or actions)

**Examples:**
- 他 (he)
- 你 (you)
- 住 (live)
- 休 (rest)
- 们 (plural marker)

---

## 2. 水 / 氵 (Water)
**Meaning:** water, liquid  
**Role:** Semantic (liquids, flow, cleansing)

**Examples:**
- 河 (river)
- 海 (sea)
- 洗 (wash)
- 酒 (alcohol)
- 流 (flow)

---

## 3. 心 / 忄 (Heart)

**Role:** Semantic (feelings, mental states)

- 情 (emotion)

---

## Summary

Most Chinese characters are **phonosemantic compounds (形声字)**:
- One part hints at **meaning** (radical)
- Another part hints at **pronunciation**

Learning these common building blocks dramatically improves:
- Vocabulary acquisition
- Character recognition
- Guessing meaning/pronunciation of new characters
