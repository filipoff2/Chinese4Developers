# Chinese Past Time Grammar Compared to Polish

This file compares the past time grammar structures between Chinese and Polish languages.

## Key Differences
- In Polish, verbs are conjugated based on the subject, while in Chinese, the verb remains the same regardless of the subject.
- Chinese relies on time adverbs and context to convey the past tense, whereas Polish has specific past tense forms for each verb.

## Examples
### Chinese
- 我昨天去商店了 (Wǒ zuótiān qù shāngdiàn le) - I went to the store yesterday.

### Polish
- Wczoraj poszedłem do sklepu - I went to the store yesterday (for males).

## Conclusion
Understanding how different languages handle past tense can greatly aid in learning and translation tasks.
